Vroom (VRM) Subsidiary Closes 25M Asset-Backed Securitization

Vroom's United Auto Credit subsidiary completes 74.9M securitization of subprime auto loans, issuing 25M in rated notes to bolster liquidity.

HOUSTON -- Vroom, Inc. (NASDAQ: VRM) disclosed in a new 8-K filing that its subsidiary, United Auto Credit Corporation (UACC), has completed a major securitization transaction to generate liquidity.

Deal Structure:

  • UACC sold a portfolio of 74.9 million in subprime motor vehicle retail installment contracts.
  • The securitization trust issued 25 million in asset-backed notes across five tranches (Class A through E).
  • Interest Rates: The blended cost of funds ranges from 4.41% for Class A notes to 7.77% for Class E notes.

Significance: This transaction provides immediate capital against Vroom's auto loan portfolio, a critical move for the company's balance sheet management.
