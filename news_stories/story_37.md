**Columbus McKinnon (CMCO) Secures $800M Strategic Investment; Appoints New Directors**

Columbus McKinnon Corporation (Nasdaq: CMCO) has successfully closed an $800 million strategic equity investment from funds managed by Clayton, Dubilier & Rice (CD&R). The transaction involved the issuance of 800,000 shares of Series A Convertible Perpetual Participating Preferred Stock at a price of $1,000 per share.

**Key Highlights:**
*   **Financing:** The $800 million proceeds are being utilized to fund the acquisition of Kito Crosby, a deal that significantly expands CMCO's lifting and securement solutions footprint.
*   **Board Appointments:** Pursuant to the Investment Agreement, the Board has appointed three CD&R designees: Michael Lamach, Nathan K. Sleeper, and Andrew Campelli.
*   **Dilution/Structure:** The preferred shares carry a 7.0% dividend rate and are convertible into common stock at an initial conversion price of $37.68 per share.

This major capital injection and governance shift marks a transformative moment for Columbus McKinnon as it integrates Kito Crosby.
