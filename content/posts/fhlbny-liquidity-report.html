FHLB New York Reports .8 Trillion Liquidity Impact

February 5, 2026 | 11:05 AM PT

NEW YORK -- The Federal Home Loan Bank of New York (FHLBNY) released a President's Report today citing a new Urban Institute study that quantifies the multiplier effect of FHLBank liquidity on the broader U.S. economy.

The study, titled The Value of the FHLBank System to Promote Housing and Community Development Lending, analyzed data from 2002 to 2024. Key findings include:

  • FHLBank advances contributed to a .8 trillion increase in combined lending by member banks and credit unions.
  • Every 00 increase in advances resulted in 8 in additional lending for banks and 7 for credit unions.
  • Post-2008 crisis, these figures rose to 8 and 6 respectively, highlighting the system's counter-cyclical role.
  • Advances supported over 50 billion in additional residential real estate lending.

FHLBNY President Randolph C. Snook stated, "The Urban study quantifies what our team, our Board and our members know to be true: that by executing on our foundational liquidity mission, the FHLBanks expand access to credit for housing, small businesses, farms and communities nationwide."

The report serves as a rebuttal to recent scrutiny of the FHLB system, emphasizing its role in stabilizing regional banking liquidity.
